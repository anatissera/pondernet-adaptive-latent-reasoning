\headerbox{Introduction}{name=resumen,column=0,row=0}{

\hspace*{0.5em} \emph{Latent chain-of-thought} models (Coconut, CODI, SIM-CoT) replace text reasoning with continuous vectors, at a much lower cost than explicit CoT. But they fix in advance the number of latent steps $K$ and the number of vectors per step $c$, applying them uniformly to every input: a two-operation problem consumes the same budget as a six-operation one. This work makes that budget \textbf{instance-adaptive}.
}

\headerbox{Objectives}{name=objetivos,column=0,below=resumen}{

\hspace*{0.5em} Make the reasoning budget \textbf{instance-adaptive}, on three fronts:
\begin{itemize}[left=0.45em, rightmargin=0.6em]\compresslist
  \item Show that the optimal budget \textbf{depends on the instance} and see whether it is predictable \emph{upfront} from the prompt.
  \item Make the number of \textbf{steps $K$} adaptive with PonderNet-style \emph{halting}, and characterize the accuracy--compute frontier that a fixed $K$ does not capture \textbf{(main contribution)}.
  \item Explore the other budget axis: the \textbf{vectors per step $c$}.
\end{itemize}
}

\headerbox{How much reasoning?}{name=opciona,column=0,below=objetivos}{

\hspace*{0.5em} We run CODI varying $k \in \{1,\dots,8\}$ over $n{=}7473$ examples and measure $k^*$ (the smallest $k$ that reaches the best accuracy for each instance).


\vspace{-0.5em}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.48\linewidth]{paperfigures/k_sweep_kstar.png}\hfill
\includegraphics[width=0.48\linewidth]{paperfigures/k_sweep_accuracy.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{-0.5em}

\hspace*{0.5em} Accuracy rises 23 points up to $k\approx5$--$6$ and then stabilizes. But the optimal $k^*$ is very \textbf{imbalanced} ($66$\,\% of instances solve with $k^*{=}1$): \textbf{there is room to adapt the budget per instance}.

\vspace{0.1em}
\hspace*{0.5em}
A classifier trained to predict $k^*$ from frozen \emph{embeddings} (MiniLM) does not beat the majority class (RF: $0.661$; LR: $0.210$). Difficulty \textbf{cannot be read \emph{upfront}}: one must decide \emph{during} reasoning.
}

\headerbox{Method: adaptive \emph{halting}}{name=metodo,column=1}{

\hspace*{0.5em} The model feeds its last hidden state back as the next input ($z_k$, ``continuous thoughts''). A single linear halting head decides, at each step, whether the accumulated reasoning is already enough to answer:
\vspace{-0.2em}
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{paperfigures/latent_reasoning_schematic.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{-0.1em}

\hspace*{0.5em} At each step, the model predicts a conditional stopping probability and a candidate answer is decoded for each prefix $k=1,\dots,K$, forming PonderNet's stopping distribution (with an absorbing boundary at $K$):
\vspace{-0.2cm}

\vspace{-0.17cm}
\[
  \lambda_k = \sigma\!\left(\texttt{halt\_head}(h_k)\right)
\]
\vspace{-0.6cm}
\[
  p_k = \big(\textstyle\prod_{j<k}(1-\lambda_j)\big)\,\lambda_k .
\]
\vspace{-0.5cm}

\hspace*{0.5em} The objective combines terms from the three methods: PonderNet (expected loss + KL regularization), SIM-CoT (per-step supervision), and CODI (distillation losses):


\vspace{-0.4cm}
\[
  \mathcal{L} = \underbrace{\textstyle\sum_k p_k\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{ans}}^{(k)}}_{\mathcal{L}_{\text{ponder}}}
  + \beta\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{step}}
  + \gamma\,\mathrm{KL}_{\text{geom}}
  + \mathcal{L}_{\text{distill}}
  + \mathcal{L}_{\text{ref}} .
\]
\vspace{-0.6cm}

\begin{itemize}[left=0.45em, rightmargin=0.6em]\compresslist
  \item The weight $\gamma$ \textbf{controls the compute}: it regulates the influence of the geometric prior and, with it, how much the reasoning chain is shortened.
  \item At inference, reasoning stops when the accumulated stopping mass exceeds a threshold (by default $0.5$); $K_{\max}$ sets the upper limit.
\end{itemize}
}



\headerbox{Per-instance geometric prior}{name=prior,column=1,below=metodo}{

\hspace*{0.5em} Instead of using a global geometric prior, we make the expected budget depend on each instance. To do so, we parametrize it as an affine function of the number of operations $n_i$:

\vspace{-0.65cm}
\[
  \texttt{geom\_mean}_i = \mathrm{clamp}\big(\alpha\, n_i + \beta,\; \beta,\; K_{\max}\big).
\]
\vspace{-0.85cm}

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{paperfigures/step_distribution.png}
\end{center}
\vspace{-0.35cm}
{\footnotesize \textbf{Figure.} Distribution of $n_i$ in GSM8k-Aug ($\mu{=}1.59$).}
}

\headerbox{Experimental setup}{name=setup,column=2}{

\begin{itemize}[left=0.45em, rightmargin=0.6em]\compresslist
  \item \textbf{Data:} GSM8k-Aug (step-by-step reasoning, Deng et al.; $\sim$385k \emph{train} examples). The training size \textbf{varies by experiment} (from the full \emph{train} set to small subsets); the models in the results band use 100k examples.
  \item \textbf{Model:} GPT-2 (124M), initialized from SIM-CoT's CODI checkpoint (\emph{warm-start}). We fine-tune the \textbf{full network} (not just adapters) together with the halting head, with a cap of $K_{\max}{=}12$ latent steps.
  \item \textbf{Evaluation:} \emph{greedy} accuracy, single pass, and \texttt{batch\_size}$=1$, on the \textbf{1319-example test set} never seen in training. The epoch and halt threshold were chosen on a separate validation set (500 examples).
  \item \textbf{Baseline (fixed $K=6$): 39.50\,\%.}
\end{itemize}
}

\headerbox{The other axis: vectors per step ($c$)}{name=opcionb,column=2,below=setup}{

\hspace*{0.5em} Each step is built with up to $M$ sub-vectors; an MLP distills the decoder's reconstruction loss ($\hat{\mathcal{L}} = \mathrm{MLP}(h)$) and survives inference: the step ends when $\hat{\mathcal{L}}$ converges.

\vspace{0.3em}
\begin{center}
\small
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{4.5pt}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Init $\times$ granularity} & $c{=}1$ & $c{=}2$ & $c{=}3$ & $\Delta_{2\rightarrow3}$ \\
\midrule
warm $+$ atomic & 27.5 & 39.4 & 39.9 & $+0.5$ \\
warm $+$ coarse & 25.5 & 36.6 & \textbf{40.3} & $+3.7$ \\
\emph{cold} $+$ coarse & 5.3 & 5.5 & 5.5 & n/a \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.2em}

\hspace*{0.5em} With atomic steps (one operation per step), accuracy \textbf{saturates at $c=2$}: adaptive stopping does not improve over random. With coarse steps (variable density), the improvement reappears (adaptive $>$ random by $2.7\sigma$), though it is small.

\textbf{The variability seems to lie in the number of steps, rather than in the complexity of each step.}
}

\headerbox{Conclusions}{name=conclu,column=2,below=opcionb}{
\vspace{0.1cm}
\begin{itemize}[left=0.45em, rightmargin=0.6em]\compresslist
    \item Learned \emph{halting} \textbf{matches the fixed-$K$ baseline} ($\approx 39.5$\,\% on test) while cutting \textbf{nearly half of the latent steps} ($3.01$ vs.\ $6$).
    \item Strong difficulty tracking: \textbf{Spearman $+0.626$} between operations and steps used (M2, test).
    \item Two informative negative results: difficulty is not readable \emph{upfront}; the $c$ axis has no headroom on GSM8k-Aug.
    \item Future work: a fair comparison training GPT-2 \textbf{from scratch} (without \emph{warm-start}, with the full train set and 40 epochs; in progress), other backbones and tasks.
\end{itemize}
}
